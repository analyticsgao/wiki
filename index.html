Fermat's Last Theorem

The 358-year-old puzzle that stumped the world's greatest minds

The Simple Question

In 1637, a French lawyer named Pierre de Fermat was reading an ancient Greek math book when he scribbled a note in the margin. That note would haunt mathematicians for over three centuries.

xn + yn = zn
has no whole number solutions when n > 2

Fermat claimed he had "a truly marvelous proof" but the margin was "too narrow to contain it." Then he died without ever writing it down. Was he bluffing? Did he actually have a proof?

The Takeaway

Fermat's Last Theorem teaches us that:
